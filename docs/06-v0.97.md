# TerraRenderer v0.97 – Perceptual Lighting

This sprint replaces the visually hard day/night blend from v0.96 with a perceptual lighting model tuned for a 466 × 466 OLED watchface.

## Changes

- Broad cubic day/night transition instead of multiplying the surface by a narrow binary-looking mask.
- Blue-grey twilight surface lift so coastlines and terrain fade naturally before reaching black.
- Separate night-light fade controls, preventing lights from appearing too close to the day side.
- Nine-tap geographic blur for city-light halos; bright urban cores retain higher dynamic range.
- Thinner, whiter atmosphere with a faster radial falloff and almost no night-side halo.
- Softer tone mapper with highlight shoulder and subtle shadow lift.
- Stronger ice highlight compression and polar suppression.
- Deeper blue oceans with less cyan and weaker specular highlights.
- Reduced relief strength to avoid an over-sharpened satellite-image appearance.

## New configuration parameters

- `twilightBandWidth`
- `twilightSurfaceLift`
- `diffusePower`
- `nightLightBlurDegrees`
- `nightLightFadeWidth`
- `nightLightFadePower`
- `atmosphere.radialFalloff`
- `atmosphere.terminatorWidth`
- `toneMapping.shadowLift`
- `toneMapping.highlightShoulder`

## Review focus

Evaluate the four standard frames primarily for:

1. Terminator softness and whether the transition still reads as a straight edge.
2. Visibility of geography in twilight without making the night side grey.
3. Polar ice dominance.
4. Whether city lights feel integrated with the globe rather than detached points.
5. Atmosphere thickness and colour.
